# Mixel.ch — Full UI Audit Report

**Date:** March 16, 2026
**Auditor:** Claude (AI-assisted)
**Scope:** Public marketing pages + portal/app (code + live site review)
**URL:** https://www.mixel.ch

---

## Executive Summary

Mixel.ch presents a professional, visually clean Swiss IT services website with strong branding fundamentals. The site structure is well-organized with clear service categorization, good typography choices (Sora + Manrope), and a consistent red/teal color palette that reinforces the Swiss identity. However, the site has several issues that undermine its effectiveness as a **first marketing touchpoint**: accessibility violations, CTA overload, content redundancy on the homepage, and a persistent contact widget that competes with conversion flows. This audit identifies **28 findings** across 7 categories, each with a concrete fix recommendation.

---

## 1. Visual Design & Branding

### 1.1 Strengths

- **Typography system is excellent.** Sora for headings + Manrope for body creates a modern, premium feel. The responsive `clamp()` sizing ensures readability across devices.
- **Color palette is cohesive.** Primary red (#dc0018) is strong and Swiss-coded. Teal accent (#00b8a9) provides good visual variety. The light backgrounds (#f8f9fa) keep things airy.
- **Card design is clean.** Consistent border-radius (0.6rem), subtle shadows, and lift-on-hover effects create a polished card system.
- **Icon system is consistent.** Lucide icons in red circles on light-red backgrounds create a recognizable pattern across all sections.

### 1.2 Issues

| # | Finding | Severity | Recommendation |
|---|---------|----------|----------------|
| V1 | **Hero section uses placeholder-style illustrations** — The "Betrieb in Bewegung" video cards, app showcase cards, and case study cards all use generic wireframe-style SVG illustrations instead of real imagery. For a marketing site, this reads as "under construction." | 🔴 High | Replace wireframe illustrations with real screenshots of your products (Accounting Workspace, Ticketsystem, etc.), actual project photos, or high-quality stock imagery. The app cards (Marketing Hub, MixeKAI, etc.) especially need real UI screenshots. |
| V2 | **Team photos use avatar silhouettes** — The "Das Team hinter der Umsetzung" section shows generic colored silhouettes instead of real headshots. This severely undermines trust for a services company. | 🔴 High | Add real team photos. For a B2B IT services company, professional headshots are table-stakes for trust. Even just 2-3 real photos would transform this section. |
| V3 | **Partner logos section is incomplete** — The technology partners grid has two empty cards (no logo/text). The Microsoft Partner card shows text only, no logo. | 🟠 Medium | Fill empty partner cards or remove them. Add the official Microsoft Partner logo. Incomplete grids signal a rushed build. |
| V4 | **Footer has no social media links** — No LinkedIn, no GitHub, nothing. For a B2B company, LinkedIn presence is expected. | 🟡 Low | Add at least a LinkedIn icon-link in the footer. |

## 2. UX Flow & Conversion

### 2.1 Strengths

- **Service catalog in hero** is a smart pattern — immediately shows what Mixel does without scrolling.
- **CostEstimator** is a strong conversion tool that lets prospects self-qualify.
- **FAQ section** addresses real objections (speed, existing systems, portal access).
- **Trilingual support** (DE/EN/FR) is well-implemented and important for Swiss market.

### 2.2 Issues

| # | Finding | Severity | Recommendation |
|---|---------|----------|----------------|
| U1 | **CTA overload — at least 6 competing CTAs visible simultaneously.** At any scroll position, users see: (1) sticky header "Strategiegespräch buchen" + "Demo anfragen", (2) hero "Jetzt anrufen" + "Strategiegespräch buchen", (3) QuickContactBar with phone + email, (4) "Jetzt anrufen" red button in header. This creates decision paralysis. | 🔴 High | Reduce to **2 primary CTAs** max visible at any time. Keep the sticky header CTAs and remove the secondary banner bar ("Brauchen Sie eine klare Roadmap…"). The QuickContactBar should only appear after scroll-depth > 50% or after 30s on page. |
| U2 | **QuickContactBar overlaps content** — The persistent bottom-right widget obscures the rightmost card in several sections (case studies, partner logos, service catalog on the right). On the pricing page, it covers the third pricing card's CTA button. | 🔴 High | Add a close/dismiss button to QuickContactBar. Reduce its width. Consider making it a collapsed icon that expands on hover/click instead of always-open. |
| U3 | **Homepage is excessively long (~15 scroll-heights).** Sections include: Hero → Stats → Trust bar → Why Mixel (4 cards) → Videos (3 cards) → Apps showcase → App details → Services grid → Case studies (3 cards) → Competence (4 cards) → Insights (3 cards) → Testimonials → Video testimonial → Team → Delivery model → Partners/Clients → Cost Estimator → CTA bar → FAQ → Contact form → Footer. This is too much for a first visit. | 🟠 Medium | Cut the homepage to 8-10 sections max. Move "Betrieb in Bewegung" videos, detailed app showcase, and full competence grid to their respective sub-pages. The homepage should tell a story: Problem → Solution → Proof → Action. |
| U4 | **Stats are shown twice** — The hero has inline badges (100+ Kunden, 17+ Jahre, 99.7% Uptime) AND there's a separate stats section with the exact same numbers in large cards right below. | 🟡 Low | Remove the inline hero badges and keep only the dedicated stats section, or vice versa. |
| U5 | **Testimonials lack credibility signals** — The customer quotes use generic names (Thomas Müller, Sarah Weber, Michael Schmidt) with no company logos, no photos, no LinkedIn links. These read as fabricated. | 🔴 High | Either use real customer quotes with verifiable attribution (company logos, real names, optional LinkedIn links), or remove the section entirely. Fake-looking testimonials damage trust more than no testimonials. |
| U6 | **Contact form has no success feedback pattern** — The form submits but there's no visible success state, loading indicator, or error handling visible in the UI. | 🟠 Medium | Add a loading spinner on submit, a success toast/message, and inline field validation errors. |
| U7 | **"Demo anfragen" CTA just opens an email** — The "Demo anfragen" button in the sticky header opens `mailto:info@mixel.ch`. This is a poor conversion path; most B2B prospects won't use mailto links. | 🟠 Medium | Replace with a link to the contact form (with "demo" pre-selected as topic) or a dedicated demo request form/page. |

## 3. Accessibility (WCAG 2.1 AA)

| # | Finding | Severity | WCAG | Recommendation |
|---|---------|----------|------|----------------|
| A1 | **Contact form inputs have no `<label>` elements** — All 5 form fields (Name, Email, Phone, Company, Message) rely on placeholder text only. Screen readers cannot identify field purpose. | 🔴 Critical | 1.3.1 | Add `<label htmlFor="...">` for every input, or at minimum `aria-label` attributes. Placeholders are not labels. |
| A2 | **CostEstimator toggle buttons missing `aria-pressed`** — The 3 module selection buttons (Security, Cloud, Support) change visual state but don't communicate toggle state to assistive technology. | 🔴 Critical | 4.1.2 | Add `aria-pressed={modules.security}` (etc.) to each toggle button. |
| A3 | **Two header buttons have no accessible name** — The locale switcher button and auth dropdown button in the header have no `aria-label`, making them announced as just "button" to screen readers. | 🟠 High | 4.1.2 | Add `aria-label="Sprache wählen"` and `aria-label="Portal-Zugang"` (translated via i18n). |
| A4 | **Service catalog hero links lack visible text in accessibility tree** — The 4 service links in the hero service catalog card are announced properly (they do have text), but the `<Link>` wrappers around the cards in other sections (app cards, case study cards) are large click targets without clear link purpose. | 🟡 Low | — | Ensure all card-links have either `aria-label` or clear heading text within. |
| A5 | **No skip-to-content link** — The site has no "Skip to main content" mechanism for keyboard users navigating past the complex header. | 🟠 High | 2.4.1 | Add a visually-hidden skip link as the first focusable element: `<a href="#main" class="sr-only focus:not-sr-only">Skip to content</a>`. |
| A6 | **No focus-visible styles on cards** — Card hover effects (lift-card) only trigger on mouse hover. Keyboard focus shows no visual indicator on card containers. | 🟠 Medium | 2.4.7 | Add `focus-visible:ring-2 focus-visible:ring-primary` to all interactive card elements. |

## 4. Responsiveness

| # | Finding | Severity | Recommendation |
|---|---------|----------|----------------|
| R1 | **Mobile nav not verified** — The viewport meta tag is correctly set, and a mobile hamburger menu exists in code, but the header navigation has many items (8 nav links + search + login + language + CTA) that may overflow on tablets (768-1024px). | 🟠 Medium | Test on iPad-width (1024px). Consider collapsing the secondary CTA bar at tablet breakpoints. Verify the hamburger menu appears early enough. |
| R2 | **QuickContactBar doesn't adapt for mobile** — The bar uses a CSS media query for viewport height (`quickbar-desktop` hidden below 760px height), but on mobile phones held in portrait the widget may still appear and consume significant screen real estate. | 🟠 Medium | Hide QuickContactBar entirely on mobile viewports (< 768px width) or collapse to a single floating phone icon. |
| R3 | **CostEstimator slider may be difficult on touch** — The Radix slider is functional but narrow; on mobile the drag target is small. | 🟡 Low | Increase slider thumb size on mobile via `@media (pointer: coarse)` to at least 44x44px (WCAG touch target). |

## 5. Copy & Messaging

### 5.1 Strengths

- Headlines are benefit-driven ("IT-Sicherheit für Schweizer KMU ohne Ausfallzeiten" is excellent).
- Sub-copy is concise and action-oriented.
- English translation is natural and well-localized (not machine-translated-sounding).

### 5.2 Issues

| # | Finding | Severity | Recommendation |
|---|---------|----------|----------------|
| C1 | **DomainGateway page has hardcoded German strings with broken umlauts** — "Waehlen Sie Ihr Ziel" instead of "Wählen Sie Ihr Ziel". At least 6 strings are not going through i18n and use ASCII-safe encoding instead of proper UTF-8. | 🟠 Medium | Move all DomainGateway strings into the i18n translation files. Fix umlaut encoding. |
| C2 | **Mixed language in navigation** — "Services" (English) appears in the German navigation alongside "Fallstudien", "Preise", "Über uns". While "Services" is common in Swiss German business context, it creates a slight inconsistency. "Apps" is also English-only. | 🟡 Low | Consider "Dienstleistungen" or keep "Services" — both are defensible. Just ensure consistency within the German locale. |
| C3 | **"Schweizer IT und Corporate Services" chip in hero is vague** — The glassmorphism badge above the H1 doesn't communicate a clear value proposition. | 🟡 Low | Replace with a more specific chip, e.g., "Seit 2009 für Schweizer KMU" or "Zertifizierter Microsoft Partner". |
| C4 | **SEO title inconsistency** — Some pages show "Mixel IT - Professionelle IT-Services in Zürich | Cybersecurity & Managed IT" (generic), while others show page-specific titles like "Managed IT Budget schätzen | Mixel IT". | 🟠 Medium | Ensure every page has a unique, keyword-rich `<title>` via the SeoHead component. The generic fallback title appears on several service pages. |

## 6. Technical & Performance

| # | Finding | Severity | Recommendation |
|---|---------|----------|----------------|
| T1 | **75+ Radix UI components bundled** — The `/components/ui/` directory imports a very large set of Radix primitives. Many may be unused on marketing pages, inflating bundle size. | 🟠 Medium | Audit actual component usage. Tree-shaking should handle this if imports are specific, but verify with `npx vite-bundle-visualizer`. Consider code-splitting the accounting module. |
| T2 | **No lazy loading on below-fold images** — The app showcase, case study, and partner logo images load eagerly. On a page this long, that's significant wasted bandwidth. | 🟠 Medium | Add `loading="lazy"` to all images below the hero fold. |
| T3 | **Google Fonts loaded synchronously** — Sora, Manrope, Space Mono, and Noto Serif are imported via CSS `@import`, which blocks rendering. | 🟠 Medium | Switch to `<link rel="preload">` with `font-display: swap` for critical fonts (Sora + Manrope), and lazy-load the others. |

| P1 | **Accounting sidebar has no responsive collapse** — The sidebar is fixed at `w-64` with no mobile treatment. On smaller screens the layout will break. | 🟠 Medium | Use the existing Radix Sidebar component which supports collapsible behavior. Add a mobile drawer pattern. |
